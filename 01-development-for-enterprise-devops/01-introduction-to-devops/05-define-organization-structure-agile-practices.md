# Define Organization Structure for Agile Practices

## Organizational Transformation Challenge
Large enterprises face:
- Rigid hierarchies slowing decision-making
- Process-heavy workflows prioritizing compliance over speed
- Risk-averse cultures discouraging experimentation
- Siloed departments optimizing locally vs. globally

**Why Change?**: Volatile business environments, startups disrupting industries, faster customer expectations

## Cultural Transformation Strategies

### From Hierarchy to Network
**Traditional**: Top-down, multiple approval layers  
**Agile**: Distributed decision-making with clear accountability

**Implementation**:
1. Push decisions down to teams
2. Establish clear boundaries for autonomy
3. Create escalation paths for out-of-scope decisions
4. Train managers as coaches, not controllers

### From Process to Outcomes
**Traditional**: Follow processes regardless of results  
**Agile**: Optimize for outcomes, adapt processes

**Key Changes**:
- Focus on business value delivery over task completion
- Measure success by customer satisfaction + business metrics
- Empower teams to modify ineffective processes
- Regular retrospectives for continuous improvement

**Principles**:
- **Team size**: 5-9 people for effective communication
- **Conway's Law**: Software architecture mirrors team structure
- **Minimize handoffs**: Each team delivers independently
- **Shared services**: Platform teams support feature teams with common needs

**Scaling Strategy**: Add entire vertical teams rather than coordinating horizontal teams
